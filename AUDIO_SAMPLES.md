# Audio Samples Guide

The placeholder audio samples in `client/public/sounds/` need to be replaced with actual royalty-free drum samples.

## Where to Get Free Drum Samples

### Free Sample Packs (Royalty-Free)

1. **Splice** (https://splice.com/sounds/free)
   - Offers free sample packs
   - High-quality 808s, kicks, snares, hi-hats

2. **Freesound** (https://freesound.org/)
   - Community-driven
   - CC-licensed samples
   - Search for specific drum sounds

3. **99Sounds** (https://99sounds.org/)
   - Free sample packs
   - No sign-up required
   - Various drum kits

4. **Bedroom Producers Blog** (https://bedroomproducersblog.com/free-samples/)
   - Curated free samples
   - Regular updates
   - Multiple genres

5. **LANDR Samples** (https://samples.landr.com/packs/category/free)
   - Free sample packs
   - Professional quality

## Required Samples

Replace the placeholder files with these actual samples:

- `kick.wav` - Punchy 808-style kick drum
- `snare.wav` - Crisp trap snare
- `hihat.wav` - Closed hi-hat
- `808.wav` - 808 bass drum (tuned to C note)
- `clap.wav` - Hand clap or trap clap
- `openhat.wav` - Open hi-hat
- `perc.wav` - Percussion (rim shot, tom, or other)
- `fx.wav` - Sound effect (vinyl scratch, riser, etc.)

## Audio Specifications

For best results:

- **Format**: WAV (uncompressed)
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz or 48kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit or 24-bit
- **Length**: 1-3 seconds (short, punchy samples work best)
- **Normalization**: Normalize to -6dB to prevent clipping

## Processing Tips

You can process samples with free tools:

1. **Audacity** (https://www.audacityteam.org/)
   - Trim, normalize, and export
   - Free and open-source

2. **Ocenaudio** (https://www.ocenaudio.com/)
   - Fast and easy to use
   - Great for batch processing
